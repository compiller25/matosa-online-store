  What the App Actually Is Right Now
  
  A local grocery ordering app for your street/neighborhood in Tanzania that:
  
  - Shows a static product list → cart → WhatsApp checkout
  - Has i18n (Swahili + English), dark/light theme, local notification stubs
  - Orders and cart reset every time the app closes (no persistence)
  - Has no backend, no real tracking, no admin panel, no payment integration
  
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  
  The Real Problem You're Solving
  
  People in your area can't easily get fresh vegetables because the market is far. Your
  app needs to make a neighbor feel confident ordering from you with these assurances:
  
  1. "My order will actually arrive" — reliable order confirmation + status
  2. "I can pay without carrying cash" — mobile money (this is Tanzania,
  M-Pesa/TigoPesa/Airtel are king)
  3. "I can trust this vendor" — business identity, reviews, clear pricing
  4. "I know what's available today" — live stock, not a static list
  
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  
  Upgrade Roadmap (Tanzania-Specific)
  
  I've broken this into 3 phases — go-to-market, growth, and scale.
  
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  
  Phase 1 — Launch Ready (Fix the Broken Fundamentals)
  
  These must be done before going to market. Without them the app feels untrustworthy.
  
  1.1 — Data Persistence
  
  Cart, orders, and UI preferences (language, theme) are lost on every app restart.
  @react-native-async-storage/async-storage is already installed. Zustand's persist
   middleware just needs to be wired in. This is a one-time 20-minute fix across 3
  stores.
  
  1.2 — Fix the Missing App Icon
  
  app.json points to ./assets/icon.png which doesn't exist. EAS build will fail
  immediately. Rename logo.png → icon.png or update the reference.
  
  1.3 — Remove the Fake Live Tracking
  
  The map shows a fake 🛵 driver that doesn't exist. This will destroy trust the first
  time a real customer sees it and wonders why the "driver" never moves. Replace it with
  a simple order status timeline (New → Confirmed → Out for Delivery → Delivered) until
  you have real delivery data.
  
  1.4 — Fix the useState Bug in HomeScreen
  
  useState(() => { fetchProducts() }) runs on every render. Change it to useEffect(() =>
  { fetchProducts() }, []). Small bug but it could cause performance problems at scale.
  
  1.5 — Compress Large Images
  
  bg001.jpg (2.8MB) and background.jpg (1.7MB) make the app feel slow. Compress to under
  200KB. This directly affects first impressions for users on slow mobile data in
  Tanzania.
  
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  
  Phase 2 — Product-Market Fit (What Makes It Actually Solve the Problem)
  
  2.1 — Mobile Money Payment (HIGHEST ROI)
  
  In Tanzania, cash on delivery has friction — the customer has to have exact change,
  the rider has to carry change. M-Pesa, TigoPesa, and Airtel Money solve this
  completely. The simplest implementation without a payment gateway:
  
  - Add payment option buttons: M-Pesa, TigoPesa, Airtel Money, Cash on Delivery
  - When customer selects mobile money, show your business Lipa Namba (business till
  number) inside the app
  - Add a "Payment Reference" field they fill after paying
  - That reference goes into the WhatsApp order message so you can verify
  - This costs you nothing to implement and works immediately without any API
  
  When you grow, you can upgrade to AzamPay or Selcom (both Tanzanian payment gateways
  with M-Pesa/TigoPesa/Airtel APIs).
  
  2.2 — Real Admin Dashboard
  
  Right now you have no way to manage your business from within the app. You need to see
  and act on incoming orders. The admin panel needs:
  
  - A PIN-locked admin screen (simple, no backend required)
  - List of all orders with status controls (New → Confirmed → Out for Delivery →
  Delivered)
  - Inventory toggle per product (In Stock / Out of Stock)
  - Simple daily sales summary (total orders, total TZS)
  
  This can be fully local (no backend) at first using AsyncStorage shared state.
  
  2.3 — Order Confirmation Flow (Fixes the #1 Customer Anxiety)
  
  Right now the flow is: customer sends WhatsApp → nothing happens on the app side. The
  customer has no idea if their order was received. Add:
  
  - An order confirmation screen that clearly shows "Order Sent, waiting for
  confirmation"
  - When you (admin) mark an order as "Confirmed" in the admin panel, the customer gets
  a local notification
  
  2.4 — Stock/Availability Status per Product
  
  The product list is static. If you run out of Mchicha today, customers still order it.
  Add an inStock: true/false flag per product, controlled from the admin panel. Show
  "Out of Stock" grayed out on the product card. This is purely local state — no backend
  needed yet.
  
  2.5 — Reorder Feature
  
  Local repeat customers (your target market) order the same items every week. A
  "Reorder" button on the Orders screen that dumps a previous order back into the cart
  removes all friction for repeat purchases.
  
  2.6 — Privacy Policy Page
  
  Google Play will reject your submission without one because you collect GPS location.
  A simple in-app screen explaining what data you collect (name, phone, location for
  delivery) is sufficient for initial submission.
  
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  
  Phase 3 — Scale (When You Have Regular Customers)
  
  3.1 — Firebase/Supabase Backend
  
  Once you have consistent daily orders, move data off-device so:
  
  - Customers see real-time stock updates
  - You can manage orders from your phone without the app being open
  - Order history survives if a customer reinstalls the app
  - Multiple delivery riders can see assigned orders
  
  Recommendation for Tanzania: Firebase (Google) — works reliably on
  Safaricom/Vodacom/Airtel data, generous free tier, no server management. Supabase is
  an alternative if you prefer SQL.
  
  3.2 — Delivery Zone Map + Dynamic Delivery Fees
  
  Draw your actual delivery coverage area on a map. When a customer pins their location,
  automatically calculate if they're in your zone and what the fee is. This prevents
  disputes and sets proper expectations.
  
  3.3 — Push Notifications via FCM
  
  When you confirm an order as admin, the customer gets a real push notification (not
  just a local one). Requires Firebase. This is what makes customers feel like they're
  using a professional service.
  
  3.4 — Product Categories + Search Improvement
  
  As your catalog grows beyond 11 items, add proper filtering: Leafy Greens,
  Tomatoes/Onions/Spices, Seasonal. The search exists but there's no category filter
  yet.
  
  3.5 — Ratings & Simple Reviews
  
  After delivery is marked complete, prompt the customer to rate (1-5 stars). This
  builds social proof for word-of-mouth growth in the neighborhood.
